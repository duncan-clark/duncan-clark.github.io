I am a current 3rd year PhD student at the University of California Los Angeles in the Statistics department. I am working on social network modelling with Professor Mark Handcock. In particular I am interested in network generation processes, Bayesian social network models and spatial point process models. I mostly use R and Python, and a little C++ for speed ups.

I am originally from the UK and after finishing a masters in Mathematics I trained to be an pensions actuary for two years, which was great grounding in basic statistics and data analysis. I've now moved over the pond after finding that I prefered tackling interesting data driven problems to calculating pensions. I have the privledge to spend my time understanding and explaining data to people and hope to make a career doing just that.
